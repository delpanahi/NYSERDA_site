Heat pumps can supply cold air in the summer and warm air in the winter.


How do heat pumps work? We're glad you asked!


  • First, they send a high-pressure liquid (refrigerant) through an expansion valve

  • Then, the refrigerant is ready to evaporate into gas and heads to the evaporator coil in the indoor unit

  • Warm indoor air then flows across the fins of the evaporator coil

  • Heat captured by the refrigerant is released, which causes the refrigerant to evaporate

  • The refrigerant gas goes back to the compressor (in the outdoor unit) and is squeezed

  • Heat that was absorbed inside home is released to outdoor air while moving across the condenser coil

  • The home is cooled!
